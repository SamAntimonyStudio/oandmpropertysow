2.The Engagement

O&M Property is launching as an independent agency across the Mornington Peninsula, stepping out from under a franchise. Antimony Studio is engaged to build the brand and take it to market, then run six months of channel management and growth, as a single 12-month partnership.

The engagement is delivered in two phases. Phase One is the launch: the brand, identity, website, collateral and channel setup that puts O&M Property live. Phase Two is the growth program: six months of channel management, content, search, lead generation and reporting that turns the new brand into enquiries, appraisals and managements across both the sales team and the rent roll.

This document sets out the scope, timeline, investment and terms of that engagement. It becomes binding on signature by both parties and receipt of the first monthly payment.

Scope of Work

3.Scope of Work

Phase One — The Launch $56,000 + GST

The complete brand and launch system, delivered ahead of the 7 November launch.

  • Strategy: competitor analysis, positioning, tone of voice and content strategy.
  • Brand identity: logo, lockup, insignia, colour, typography, full design system and brand guidelines.
  • Website: custom design and development, integrated with the tech stack, CRM and channels, with listings feed, agent profiles and enquiry capture.
  • Print, collateral & signage: stationery, welcome pack, brochures, for-sale boards and rental signage, print-ready for suppliers. Size and format variants as specified, not print quantities.
  • Channel setup: channel design, introduction and link building, plus content templates for Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook and Google.

Phase Two — The Growth Program · 6 months $22,000 + GST

Six months of channel management and growth, built for an agency running both a rent roll and a sales team.

  • Channel management: ongoing, on-brand posting and community management across Instagram, LinkedIn, Meta and Google.
  • Content engine: community content covering local businesses, events, schools and area stories.
  • Search & visibility: local SEO, Google Business Profile and high-intent paid search management.
  • Lead generation & lifecycle: website capture, CRM automation, SMS and email nurture across buyers, vendors and the rent roll.
  • Founder & agent brand: founder profile support and the foundation for agent sub-brands.
  • Reporting & optimisation: monthly data-driven reporting tied to rent roll and sales performance.

Monthly allocation. The growth program fee covers the following per calendar month: up to twelve (12) social posts across channels; two (2) community content pieces; management of Google Business Profile and one (1) paid search campaign (media spend excluded); up to four (4) lifecycle flows built in the first two months and maintained thereafter; four (4) founder profile posts; and one (1) monthly performance report. Work beyond this allocation is a Change Request under clause 7.7 and requires written approval before it proceeds.

Optional — Office Signage $3,700 + GST

Not included unless elected in writing. Front desk, frontage and portable bifold sign, design and print-ready artwork only. Production and delivery are not included and are quoted separately by the signage supplier.

Terms & Conditions

7.Terms & Conditions

7.1 Antimony Studio Terms & Conditions — incorporation

The Antimony Studio Terms & Conditions ("the Terms") are provided to the Client together with this Statement of Work, before the engagement is formed, and are incorporated into and form part of this agreement. The Client acknowledges receipt of the Terms at clause 8 below. This Statement of Work and the Terms are to be read together. Where a provision of this Statement of Work conflicts with the Terms, this Statement of Work prevails to the extent of that conflict.

7.2 Commencement & term

This agreement commences on the effective date and continues for twelve (12) months from the kickoff workshop, unless terminated in accordance with clause 7.12. The engagement is accepted, and the Project commences, on receipt of this signed Statement of Work and the first monthly payment.

7.3 Fees, GST & payment

All fees are stated exclusive of GST unless marked otherwise, and GST is payable in addition. Invoices are issued monthly in advance and, in accordance with clause 16.2 of the Terms, are payable within fourteen (14) days of receipt. Late payments incur interest at 5.0% per month on the overdue amount, calculated from the due date, under clause 16.3 of the Terms. The Studio may suspend work on any overdue account until it is brought current.

7.4 Intellectual property

All final deliverables produced under this agreement become the property of the Client upon full payment of all associated fees, in accordance with clause 5.1 of the Terms. Pre-existing tools, templates, systems and proprietary methodologies remain the exclusive intellectual property of the Studio. The Studio retains the right to use the non-confidential parts of the work for portfolio, awards and promotional purposes.

7.5 Client responsibilities

The Client will nominate a single decision-making representative for the duration of the engagement, provide timely access to stakeholders, information and brand materials, and review and approve feedback requests within three (3) business days unless otherwise agreed. The Client must notify the Studio within seven (7) days of receiving a deliverable whether it is accepted or requires rectification. Delays in feedback, approvals or the provision of materials may move the timeline and are not the responsibility of the Studio.

7.6 Revisions

Each major deliverable includes two (2) rounds of consolidated revisions. Further rounds, or changes of direction after written approval of a deliverable, are treated as a Change Request under clause 7.7.

Terms & Conditions

7.7 Scope, Change Requests & out-of-scope work

The scope of this engagement is defined at clause 3. Anything outside it is a Change Request, governed by section 7 of the Terms. Before performing any work beyond the agreed scope, the Studio will notify the Client in writing, provide an estimate of the additional time and cost, and obtain the Client's written approval. No out-of-scope work will proceed, and no out-of-scope work will be invoiced, without that prior written approval.

Written approval means an email, or a written confirmation on the Client's nominated project channel, from the Client's nominated decision-maker. A verbal request alone is not approval for the purposes of this clause. Approved out-of-scope work is charged at $200.00 + GST per hour, or as otherwise agreed in writing.

7.8 Growth program allocation & reporting

The growth program fee covers the monthly allocation set out at clause 3. The Studio will report on delivery against that allocation each month. Where the Studio identifies that the work required in a month is likely to exceed the allocation, it will follow the notification and written approval process at clause 7.7 before proceeding. Any overage reported in a monthly report that is not disputed by the Client within seven (7) business days of that report is deemed accepted, in accordance with clause 7.7 of the Terms.

7.9 Third-party costs & media spend

Third-party costs are excluded from the fees in this agreement and are funded by the Client, in accordance with clause 16.5 of the Terms. These include printing, production, fabrication and installation of signage, fonts, stock imagery, subscriptions, platform and disbursement costs, subcontractors, travel, and all advertising and media spend. Advertising and media budgets are set and approved by the Client.

7.10 Confidentiality

Each party will keep the other's confidential information private and use it only for the purposes of this engagement, both during the term and after it ends, in accordance with section 9 of the Terms.

7.11 Pausing

Either party may pause the project on ten (10) business days' written notice. During a pause, a retainer fee of AUD $2,000 per month is payable to retain project resources. A pause exceeding four (4) weeks may require a new delivery schedule or scope revision.

Terms & Conditions

7.12 Termination & Phase One protection

Either party may terminate this agreement on thirty (30) days' written notice. On termination, the Client will be invoiced for all work completed to date, all pre-approved expenses will be reimbursed, and any approved out-of-scope work will be invoiced at the rate in clause 7.7.

This clause is agreed in place of the early termination fee at clause 8.3 of the Terms, as expressly permitted by that clause. Phase One — the Launch — carries a fee of $56,000 + GST and is built and delivered ahead of full payment under the ramped monthly schedule at clause 6. The Phase One fee is earned in full on delivery of Phase One. If this agreement is terminated by either party after Phase One has been delivered, the entire unpaid balance of the $56,000 + GST Phase One fee becomes immediately due and payable within thirty (30) days of the agreed departure date, together with the fee for any growth program work performed or committed to that date. This clause survives termination.

7.13 Authorised work & clause 13.1 of the Terms

Clause 13.1 of the Terms is varied for this engagement and applies as follows. Neither party is liable for variations to the Services made by the other party without authorisation, or for misuse of the Deliverables outside its reasonable control. That clause concerns liability only. It does not limit the Client's obligation to pay for work the Client has requested, directed or approved.

For the purposes of this agreement, work is authorised where the Client's nominated decision-maker requests, directs or approves it in writing, including by email or on the nominated project channel. Where the Client authorises work in writing and the Studio delivers it in good faith and records it in the monthly report, that work is authorised and payable, whether or not a formal Change Request was raised. Work the Studio performs beyond the agreed scope without the Client's written authorisation is not payable by the Client.

7.14 Non-solicitation

During the term and for twelve (12) months after it ends, neither party will solicit or hire the other's employees, contractors or collaborators involved in the project without the other's prior written consent, in accordance with section 11 of the Terms.
